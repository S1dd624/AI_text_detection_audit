Hybrid BiLSTM for AI-Generated Text Detection
Motivation and Problem Context
The proliferation of large language models capable of generating fluent prose has fundamentally altered written communication. Systems like GPT-4 and Claude produce text virtually indistinguishable from human writing through casual inspection. While enabling productivity gains, this capability raises critical concerns regarding content authenticity, academic integrity, misinformation propagation, and preservation of human creative voice.
The scale and speed of AI prose generation vastly exceed human capacity. A single model can generate thousands of words in seconds, threatening to overwhelm human-generated material across domains from product descriptions to news articles. Traditional detection approaches based on grammatical errors or stylistic quirks fail against modern LLMs that have internalized sophisticated language patterns.
This research addresses AI text detection through a hybrid approach combining explicit linguistic feature engineering with deep learning sequence modeling. Rather than treating detection as pure black-box classification, the methodology embraces interpretability by extracting meaningful linguistic characteristics and augmenting them with a Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory network capable of capturing sequential dependencies.
The fast fashion domain serves as investigation context due to its distinctive characteristics. Fashion writing exhibits heightened flair, emotional resonance, and persuasive intent. Product descriptions employ vivid vocabulary, exclamatory emphasis, and aspirational framing designed to evoke desire. These patterns provide rich ground for distinguishing human creative expression from AI-generated alternatives.
Architectural Design and Model Structure
The detection system integrates two complementary information streams: explicitly computed linguistic features and learned sequential representations from the BiLSTM network.
Linguistic Feature Engineering (5-Dimensional Vector)
Five carefully selected features comprise the explicit linguistic characterization:
Expanse of Prose: Quantifies vocabulary richness as the ratio of unique words to total word count. Human writers, particularly in creative domains, employ varied vocabulary to maintain engagement. AI systems sometimes exhibit repetitive word choice patterns due to probability-driven generation.
Exclamation Density: Raw count of exclamation marks normalized by sentence count. Fashion marketing employs exclamatory sentences to convey enthusiasm and urgency. The normalized density reveals whether usage aligns with human patterns or exhibits AI characteristics.
Question Prevalence: Question marks normalized by sentence count. Rhetorical questions serve as engagement devices in marketing copy. The balance of interrogative sentences provides stylistic fingerprints.
Emotion Word Count: Tallies words from a curated emotion lexicon encompassing terms expressing positive and negative affect (beautiful, stunning, disappointing). Fashion writing inherently discusses aesthetic judgment, making emotion vocabulary central to authentic expression.
Personalizability: Frequency of first-person and second-person pronouns (I, we, you, your) normalized by word count. Fashion marketing often directly addresses readers or expresses brand voice through first-person framing. AI-generated text sometimes exhibits systematic differences in personal pronoun usage.
These five features form a 5-dimensional vector with clear interpretability—analysts can understand what each dimension measures and why it distinguishes human from AI writing.
BiLSTM Network Architecture
The neural component consists of a Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory network for sequential data processing. Text exhibits sequential structure; word order, phrase construction, and sentence flow all carry meaning. The BiLSTM processes text in both directions: one LSTM reads forward (left-to-right), another reads backward (right-to-left). At each position, the network accesses both past and future context.
The implemented architecture begins with a 64-dimensional embedding layer transforming discrete word tokens into continuous vector representations. The BiLSTM layer comprises 64 hidden units in each direction, yielding a 128-dimensional combined representation. This hidden unit count balances capacity to capture complex patterns against overfitting risks.
