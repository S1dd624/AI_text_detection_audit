Abstract— The Emotion-Aware Chatbot is an AI-driven mental
health companion designed to offer emotional support to
individuals experiencing loneliness, stress, or depressive
tendencies, particularly students at risk of suicide. By integrating
facial emotion detection using OpenCV with a sentiment-aware
chatbot built on the Chatterbot framework, the system identifies
a user’s emotional state in real-time and responds empathetically
to their needs. The chatbot not only engages in natural language
conversation but also adapts its responses based on the user’s
visual and textual sentiment cues, creating a more personalized
and comforting interaction. Built using Flask for web
integration, the system is lightweight and deployable on local or
institutional servers. It serves as a virtual mental health assistant
that can be integrated into therapy centers, schools, universities,
and smart wellness platforms. Future enhancements include
multilingual support, wearable integration, and emergency alert
systems via APIs like Twilio. This chatbot represents a scalable,
proactive approach to mental wellness and suicide prevention
using AI.

Mental health disorders, particularly Social Anxiety
Disorder (SAD) and depression, are emerging as critical
public health concerns in the modern digital age. The World
Health Organization (WHO) classifies mental disorders as
conditions characterized by disturbances in thinking, emotion
regulation, or behavior, often coupled with distress and
impaired functioning [1]. SAD affects millions globally, with
an estimated 12.1% lifetime prevalence [2], manifesting as
an intense fear of social interactions and being negatively
evaluated. Despite the growing need, over 75%, that is more
than three fourths of people in countries that are in the low-
middle income range do not receive any treatment for mental
health issues due to social stigma, lack of accessibility, or
affordability [3].

To address these challenges, Emotion-Aware Chatbots offer
a transformative solution by bridging the gap between
individuals and mental health support. These AI-driven
systems employ natural language processing (NLP),
computer vision, and deep learning to interpret user
sentiment and facial expressions in real-time, enabling them
to deliver personalized emotional support. This project
presents a Flask-based web application that integrates
OpenCV for emotion detection and Chatterbot for
conversational AI, supported by a user database for
emotional trend tracking. The chatbot can understand
emotional states through both text and visual cues and
responding empathetically, simulating a human-like
supportive interaction. Through continuous use, it helps detect
emotional deterioration patterns and can serve as a frontline
tool for early intervention and emotional self-awareness,
particularly in academic, clinical, or personal environments.
This approach not only reduces the burden on healthcare
infrastructure but also provides a scalable and stigma-free
means of support [4].
Recent advancements in affective computing and human-
computer interaction (HCI) have further enabled machines to
understand and respond to human emotions with increasing
precision. Emotional intelligence in AI systems is now being
used not only in commercial domains but also in mental health
interventions, where responsiveness to non-verbal cues like
facial expressions plays a crucial role [5]. Chatbots equipped
with emotion-sensing capabilities have demonstrated
promising results in reducing feelings of isolation, encouraging
emotional expression, and guiding users toward helpful
resources [6]. Moreover, the integration of AI with healthcare
tools aligns with the global push towards tele-mental health,
especially post-COVID-19, where remote yet meaningful
support became essential. By combining text-based sentiment
analysis with visual emotion recognition, our chatbot system
represents a hybrid model capable of continuous emotional
monitoring and intervention, making it a potential digital ally
for those battling emotional distress in silence.

A. Dataset
The proposed emotion-aware chatbot system is built upon a

Our
preprocessing strategy is distinctly tailored to the nature of the
data, encompassing both textual and visual components. For
textual data, user inputs are first normalized by converting all
characters to lowercase, stripping punctuation, and removing
stop words, which helps reduce noise and standardize the text
for further analysis. We then apply tokenization using the
Natural Language Toolkit (NLTK) to segment the text into
individual words or meaningful units, facilitating a structured
representation of the data. These tokens are transformed into
numerical vectors using Term Frequency–Inverse Document
Frequency (TF-IDF), a widely used technique that weighs the
importance of a term in a document relative to its occurrence in
the entire corpus. The model is thus able to diffrentiate relevant
emotional cues while downplaying ubiquitous or less
informative words [16]. The resulting TF-IDF vectors are stored
and utilized as input for classifier training.
For visual data, real-time frames are captured through a webcam
using the OpenCV library. 
Altogether, these preprocessing steps enhance model
robustness, reduce bias, and ensure consistency across
modalities while minimizing the risk of data leakage.

C. Feature Selection
Feature selection is a crucial step in building efficient and
interpretable machine learning models, especially when
dealing with high-dimensional data in multimodal emotion
recognition systems. In this study, tailored feature selection
approaches were employed for both textual and visual
emotion domains to reduce computational complexity,
enhance interpretability, and improve classifier performance.
By systematically identifying and retaining only the most
relevant features, the models became more robust and less
prone to overfitting, ultimately leading to more accurate
emotional predictions.
For the textual domain, the initial representation of data was
achieved through Term Frequency-Inverse Document
Frequency (TF-IDF) vectorization, which converts raw text into
a numerical format while preserving the relative importance of
each word in the corpus. Given the large number of generated
features, we employed the Chi-square (χ²) test to evaluate the
independence between each word and the sentiment label
(positive or negative). Features with higher Chi-square scores
indicated a stronger relationship with the target classes and were
therefore retained. 
Overall, these combined
strategies contributed to a refined, multimodal emotion
recognition pipeline with improved transparency and predictive

This intelligent system bridges the gap between emotional
distress and timely support, especially for individuals
suffering from conditions like Social Anxiety Disorder
(SAD). By analysing user behaviour, facial expressions, and
mood patterns, Emoticon.AI proactively identifies signs of
mental health deterioration. Its AI-driven chatbot offers non-
judgmental companionship, easing the burden of loneliness and
fear of stigma.
One of the greatest advantages of Emoticon.AI is its potential to
intervene during critical emotional lows, offering real-time
reassurance, emergency alerts, and therapeutic suggestions. This
system can play a pivotal role in suicide prevention by being a
readily available, emotionally aware companion. In a world
growing increasingly isolated, Emoticon.AI stands as a digital
ally fostering connection, empathy, and early mental health care.
Furthermore, Emoticon.AI’s integration with healthcare
databases and support networks empowers it to provide tailored
recommendations and connect users with mental health
professionals when necessary. Its continuous learning
capabilities enable the system to evolve with user interactions,
ensuring increasingly accurate emotional assessments. By
promoting self-awareness and encouraging timely intervention,
Emoticon.AI not only supports mental wellness but also builds
a sustainable, tech-enabled framework for long-term emotional
resilience.
